# Privacy Policy — Killer Sudoku by Seventh Chronicle

**Last updated:** 11 April 2026

## Who we are

Killer Sudoku ("the App") is published by Seventh Chronicle Ltd ("we", "us"). This privacy policy explains what data the App collects, how it is used, and the choices available to you depending on where you live.

For any privacy question, contact: dark.disc6864@fastmail.com

## What data we collect

### Analytics

The App does not collect analytics. No gameplay, screen-view, feature-usage, or purchase-flow data is sent off your device.

Your game state — saved puzzles, preferences, statistics, streak records, and puzzle progress — is stored exclusively on your device using an on-device database and is never transmitted anywhere. Uninstalling the App deletes this data.

### Advertising

The App shows advertisements served by **Google AdMob**. AdMob is Google's mobile advertising platform, and as such Google processes personal data — including device identifiers (such as the Android Advertising ID), IP address, coarse location inferred from IP, and ad interaction history — in order to serve ads to you. The details of Google's processing are governed by Google's own privacy policy at https://policies.google.com/privacy.

Premium users who purchase the ad-free upgrade will no longer see ads and the AdMob SDK will not serve ad requests for them.

### Purchases

If you choose to purchase a Premium upgrade, the purchase itself is processed by **Google Play Billing**. We do not receive or store your payment card details; Google Play handles the transaction. We receive only the purchase token needed to validate your entitlement. Google's handling of purchase data is governed by [Google's Privacy Policy](https://policies.google.com/privacy).

## Consent in regulated regions

Depending on where you are, the App will show different consent dialogs the first time you launch it (and whenever you tap **Settings → Privacy options**):

- **European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, and Switzerland:** A GDPR / IAB TCF consent dialog managed by **Google's User Messaging Platform (UMP)**, a Google-certified Consent Management Platform. You must make a choice before the App will serve you ads. You may grant or withdraw consent to personalized advertising at any time via **Settings → Privacy options**.

- **Listed US states (California, Colorado, Connecticut, Virginia, Utah, Oregon, Texas, and others as state laws evolve):** A US state regulations notice allowing you to opt out of the sale or sharing of your personal information by Google AdMob, as required by CCPA/CPRA and state-specific privacy laws. You may change your choice at any time via **Settings → Privacy options**.

- **Other regions:** No consent dialog is shown and no equivalent in-app mechanism is provided, because no applicable law mandates one.

The consent mechanism in both regulated cases is implemented using Google's User Messaging Platform, which is a Google-certified CMP that links directly to this privacy policy from within the consent dialog.

## Data transfers

AdMob-served ads may transfer personal data to Google in the **United States** under the standard Google Ads data-processing terms. Google is a self-certified recipient under the **EU-US Data Privacy Framework** (and its UK Extension and the Swiss-US Data Privacy Framework), which provides a recognised transfer mechanism for personal data flowing from the EEA, the UK, and Switzerland to participating US organisations.

## Legal basis for processing

- **EEA / United Kingdom / Switzerland:** Explicit user consent under **GDPR Article 6(1)(a)** (and the equivalent provisions of the UK GDPR and the Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection), collected via the UMP consent dialog described above. No personalized advertising data is processed until that consent is granted, and you may withdraw it at any time.
- **Listed US states:** Opt-out model under **CCPA/CPRA** and equivalent state laws (Colorado Privacy Act, Connecticut Data Privacy Act, Virginia CDPA, Utah Consumer Privacy Act, Oregon Consumer Privacy Act, Texas Data Privacy and Security Act, and others as they come into force), surfaced via the US states regulations notice.
- **Other regions:** Legitimate interest (GDPR Article 6(1)(f) or the equivalent local provision) for operating an ad-funded free app, balanced against the limited nature of the processing.

## Privacy options

You can change your advertising consent choice at any time by opening the App and going to **Settings → Privacy options**. This re-presents the same consent dialog that appeared on first launch and allows you to grant, deny, or modify your choices. If you are outside a regulated region the entry point is hidden because no equivalent mechanism applies.

## Data the App does NOT collect

Beyond the advertising-related processing described above, the App does not collect, request, or store:

- Your name, email address, or contact information
- Your precise location
- Photos, files, or media
- Health, financial, or biometric data
- Contacts or call logs
- Browsing history outside of the App

No account or login is required to use the App.
